Welcome to Xavier!

Xavier is a user-friendly tool for OLAP data analysis and visualisation. Xavier can be used in combination with any XML/A (XML for Analysis) server, like Mondrian, Pentaho, icCube, Microsoft Analysis Services and others.

Getting started with Xavier

Using Xavier is simple:

  1. Click one of the available cubes from the list at the left-hand side of the screen to select it.
  2. Create a visualization tab page by choosing a visualization type, like table, pivot table, or a chart type (like a pie chart or bar chart) by clicking the corresponding button on the main toolbar at the top of the screen.
  3. Choose measures and categorical items (like hierarchies, hierarchy levels or hierarchy level properties, or individual members) from the cube and drag them from the left-hand side of the screen to the visualization tab at the right hand side of the screen.
  4. Use the interactive capabilities of the visualization to make selections or to drill up or down.

That's it! You can create as many visualizations as you like. When you're done creating visualizations, you can save them or print them.

You can also export your visualization to Microsoft Excel for further processing or distribution.

Inline help

Most elements of the Xavier user interface have associated tooltips that contain additional information. To reveal the tooltip infomation, hover and pause your pointing device (mouse) over the item. If the item has an associated tooltip it will fade into view within a second or so.

External documentation

The author of your OLAP database may also have attached links to online documentation to various elements like cubes, dimensions and so on; If that is the case, then an information icon will be present after the label of the item. Clicking the information icon will follow the link and open a new tab page where the additional documentation should appear.

If you have trouble opening such external documentation, then please contact the author of your OLAP database.
