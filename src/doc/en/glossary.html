Glossary

Bar chart
A bar chart graphically represents categories as bars that are sized according to the value of its associated measure. Read more about bar charts in wikipedia.
Catalog
A catalog is a collection of cubes. Usually, the cubes in a catalog are related in terms of content or target audience.
Hierarchy
A hierarchy is a collection of categorical values, ordered by aggregation level. For example, time is hierarchically ordered into levels of year, quarter, month, and so on. Read more about hierarchies in wikipedia.
Level
A level of aggregation within a hierarchy.
Cube
A data structure suitable for quick analysis of business process data. Read more about cubes in wikipedia.
Member
A member is a single categorical value within a level of a hierarchy. For example, "2015" is a member of the year level within a time hierarchy.
Measure
Measures (also known as facts) are quantitative data describing a business process. For example, a sales process typically as sale quantity and sale price as measures. Read more about measures in wikipedia.
OLAP
OLAP is an abbreviation of Online Analytical Processing. OLAP is mostly used for quickly analysing and reporting business process data on an ad-hoc basis . Read more about OLAP in wikipedia.
Pie chart
A pie chart graphically represents categories of one series of measures as sectors ("slice") of a circle. Read more about pie charts in wikipedia.
Pivot table
A pivot table (also known as crosstab) is a kind of table in which both column and row headers represent a group of data. At the intersection of the column and row headers are cells containing an aggregated value calculated from the underlying measuresa>. Read more about pivot tables in wikipedia.
Property
Properties are additional data items or fields that are typically defined for levels. The actual values of the properties are available for the members that belong to the level. For example, a product level in a product hierarchy can hold useful bits of information like the product price. Properties can also be used to store alternative or additional labels for the member, for example, an abbreviated caption or a longer description text.
Tabel
A means of organizing data in a collection of rows having a fixed column structure. Read more about tables in wikipedia.
